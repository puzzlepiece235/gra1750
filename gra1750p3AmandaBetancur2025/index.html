Meringue Cookies

These delicate meringue cookies are the perfect treat for anyone who loves a melt-in-your-mouth dessert. Made from just a handful of simple ingredients—egg whites, sugar, and a pinch of vanilla—these cookies are light as air and have a delightful, crisp texture. With a subtle sweetness and a soft, airy interior, each bite is a little cloud of bliss. Perfect for snacking, gifting, or serving alongside tea or coffee, these meringue cookies are both elegant and easy to make. Whether you're a seasoned baker or a beginner, this recipe is sure to impress. With their charming appearance and satisfying crunch, meringue cookies are a wonderful way to add a touch of sweetness to any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 3 Large white eggs
  • ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon cream of tartar (optional)
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 185° Farenheit
  2. Combine egg whites, sugar, and cream of tartar in a heat-proof bowl.
  3. Place the bowl over a pot pf simmering water and whisk the mixture until it reaches 120° Fahrenheit
  4. Remove the mixture and continue to beat on high speed until the meringue holds stiff peaks.
  5. Place the meringue into piping bags fitted with a coupler and a tip 1M.
  6. Pipe rosettes on parchement-lined baking sheets.
  7. Bake the meringue cookies at 185° Fahrenheit for 2 hours. Allow them to cool before removing them from the parchment paper.
  8. Store the meringue cookies in an airtight container layered with parchment paper at room temperature for up to 5 days or in the freezer for up to 3 months.
